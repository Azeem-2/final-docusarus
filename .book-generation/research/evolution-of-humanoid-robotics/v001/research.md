# Topic: Evolution of Humanoid Robotics

**Research Date**: 2025-12-01

**Total Sources**: 10 (6 Tier 1, 4 Tier 2)

## Research Question

How have humanoid robots evolved from early concepts and laboratory prototypes to today’s commercially-focused platforms (e.g., Optimus, Figure 01, Apollo, Atlas), and what technical, economic, and societal factors define \"generations\" in humanoid robotics?

## Key Findings

1. Humanoid robotics has progressed through several phases: early mechanical/animatronic systems, research‑grade walkers (e.g., Honda’s ASIMO), platform‑oriented research (e.g., HRP, Nao), dynamic humanoids (e.g., Atlas), and current \"general‑purpose\" commercial efforts (Optimus, Figure 01, Apollo). – Confidence: High  
2. The main technical drivers across generations include advances in actuators (from geared motors to custom torque‑dense actuators), sensing (IMUs, force/torque sensors, vision), control (from ZMP-based walking to whole‑body control and model predictive control), and now AI/ML for perception and behavior. – Confidence: High  
3. Historically, progress was constrained by cost, fragility, and limited commercial use‑cases; recent interest is fueled by labor shortages, demographic shifts, and high‑profile companies committing to mass production. – Confidence: High  
4. From an educational perspective, organizing the history into 4–5 \"eras\" helps students see the interplay between research breakthroughs, enabling technologies, and market narratives. – Confidence: High
