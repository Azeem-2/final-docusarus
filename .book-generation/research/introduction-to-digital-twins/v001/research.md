## Research Question

What are **digital twins**, how do they differ from traditional simulation models, and how are they used in robotics and manufacturing contexts that students will encounter later in this book?

## Key Findings

1. A digital twin is a **living digital representation** of a physical asset, process, or system that is continuously updated with real‑time or near‑real‑time data. – Confidence: High  
2. Digital twins differ from offline simulations by being **bi‑directionally connected** to the physical world (data flows from physical to digital; insights and control commands can flow back). – Confidence: High  
3. Types of digital twins include **component/asset twins**, **system/process twins**, and **environment twins**, each serving different purposes (design, monitoring, optimization, training). – Confidence: High  
4. In manufacturing and robotics, digital twins support predictive maintenance, throughput optimization, safety analysis, operator training, and what‑if scenario exploration. – Confidence: High  
5. Market analyses project rapid growth in digital twin adoption across industries, positioning them as a core enabling technology for Industry 4.0 and beyond. – Confidence: High
