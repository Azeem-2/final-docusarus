## Lesson 1: Analysis Framework and Tesla Optimus

- **Pedagogical Layer**: Layer 1–2 – Intuition & Concepts  
- **Estimated Time**: 90–120 minutes  
- **Learning Outcomes**:  
  1. Understand case study analysis framework.  
  2. Analyze Tesla Optimus technical specifications and capabilities.  
  3. Connect Optimus features to Part 5 concepts.

### Parts 1–6

- **Hook**: How do real-world humanoid robots implement the concepts we've learned? Let's analyze Tesla Optimus.  
- **Theory**:  
  - **Analysis framework**:  
    - Hardware specifications: Size, weight, actuators, sensors.  
    - Locomotion: Walking, balance, terrain adaptation.  
    - Manipulation: Hands, grasping, dexterity.  
    - HRI: Communication, interaction.  
    - Safety: Safety features and mechanisms.  
    - AI/Control: Control architecture, learning methods.  
  - **Tesla Optimus**:  
    - General purpose bipedal humanoid.  
    - Tesla-designed actuators and sensors.  
    - Gen 2 improvements: Faster walking, better hands.  
    - Autonomous operation, AI-powered control.  
    - Production plans: 2025, Tesla facilities.  
- **Walkthrough**:  
  - Show analysis framework: systematic approach to analyzing robots.  
  - Analyze Optimus: hardware → locomotion → manipulation → AI.  
  - Connect to Part 5: How Optimus implements concepts from earlier chapters.  
- **Challenge**:  
  - Students analyze Optimus:  
    1. Apply analysis framework.  
    2. Identify key technical features.  
    3. Connect features to Part 5 concepts.  
- **Takeaways**:  
  - Analysis framework enables systematic robot evaluation.  
  - Optimus demonstrates mass production approach to humanoids.  
  - Real-world implementations inform design choices.  
- **Learn with AI**:  
  - `robot_analyzer`: RI component that helps students analyze humanoid robots using the framework.

## Lesson 2: Figure 01 and Boston Dynamics Atlas

- **Pedagogical Layer**: Layer 2–3 – Concepts & Application  
- **Estimated Time**: 90–120 minutes  
- **Learning Outcomes**:  
  1. Analyze Figure 01 technical specifications and capabilities.  
  2. Analyze Boston Dynamics Atlas technical specifications and capabilities.  
  3. Compare different design approaches.

### Parts 1–6

- **Hook**: Optimus takes one approach, but Figure 01 and Atlas take different paths. How do they differ?  
- **Theory**:  
  - **Figure 01**:  
    - OpenAI-powered conversational humanoid.  
    - Human-like dimensions, 20 kg lifting capacity.  
    - Speech-to-speech reasoning, conversational AI.  
    - AI-first design philosophy.  
  - **Boston Dynamics Atlas**:  
    - Dynamic humanoid research platform.  
    - Advanced actuators, dynamic motions.  
    - Reinforcement learning, whole-body manipulation.  
    - Research-focused design philosophy.  
- **Walkthrough**:  
  - Analyze Figure 01: AI integration → conversational capabilities → applications.  
  - Analyze Atlas: RL control → dynamic motions → manipulation.  
  - Compare approaches: Different design philosophies and trade-offs.  
- **Challenge**:  
  - Students compare robots:  
    1. Analyze Figure 01 and Atlas.  
    2. Identify design differences.  
    3. Explain trade-offs.  
- **Takeaways**:  
  - Figure 01 emphasizes AI and conversation.  
  - Atlas emphasizes dynamic capabilities and research.  
  - Different design philosophies suit different applications.  
- **Learn with AI**:  
  - `robot_comparator`: RI component that helps students compare different humanoid robots.

## Lesson 3: Comparative Analysis and Lessons Learned

- **Pedagogical Layer**: Layer 3–4 – Application & Integration  
- **Estimated Time**: 90–120 minutes  
- **Learning Outcomes**:  
  1. Compare all three robots across multiple dimensions.  
  2. Understand design trade-offs and implications.  
  3. Extract lessons learned for future humanoid design.

### Parts 1–6

- **Hook**: We've analyzed three robots. What can we learn from comparing them?  
- **Theory**:  
  - **Comparative analysis**:  
    - Design philosophy: Mass production vs AI-first vs research.  
    - Capabilities: Locomotion, manipulation, HRI, safety.  
    - Applications: Industrial, conversational, research.  
  - **Lessons learned**:  
    - Design choices: What works, what doesn't.  
    - Technology trade-offs: Different approaches and implications.  
    - Future directions: Where humanoid robotics is heading.  
- **Walkthrough**:  
  - Compare all three: design → capabilities → applications.  
  - Extract lessons: What we can learn from each approach.  
  - Discuss future: Where the field is heading.  
- **Challenge**:  
  - Students synthesize lessons:  
    1. Compare all three robots.  
    2. Identify key lessons.  
    3. Predict future directions.  
- **Takeaways**:  
  - Different design philosophies suit different applications.  
  - Technology trade-offs inform design choices.  
  - Case studies inform future humanoid design.  
- **Learn with AI**:  
  - `lessons_synthesizer`: RI component that helps students extract lessons from case studies.
