## Pass 3: Consistency and Explicit Citations

### Terminology and Style

- Use of “physical AI”, “embodied intelligence”, and “robotics vs AI” is consistent with P1‑C1 and project constitution.  
- Perspective and tone match the rest of the manuscript (second person where appropriate, expert but encouraging).  

### In‑Text Source References (Minor Revisions)

- The draft references “classical AI textbooks”, “survey articles on embodied cognition”, and “historical overviews” in generic terms.  
- To strengthen academic grounding, add 2–3 explicit, short attributions, for example:  
  - “Classical AI texts such as [name of standard textbook] define AI as…”  
  - “Embodied cognition work by [author] emphasizes the role of body and environment…”  
  - “Historical accounts of robotics and AI (e.g., [author]/[survey]) trace these separate roots and convergence.”

These can remain high‑level and do not require full citation formatting in the body, as long as the bibliography later contains proper entries.
