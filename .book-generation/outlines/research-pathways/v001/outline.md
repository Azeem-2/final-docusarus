## Section 4: Key Terms

**Word Count**: 400-600 words
**Purpose**: Define essential terminology for navigating research pathways.

### Terms (15-20 definitions):
1. **PhD Program** - Doctoral degree requiring original research, comprehensive exams, thesis defense
2. **Research Assistantship (RA)** - Funding through faculty research projects
3. **Teaching Assistantship (TA)** - Funding through course support
4. **Fellowship** - Competitive award providing full funding without work requirement
5. **Postdoctoral Position** - Research role after PhD, before faculty/industry
6. **Research Lab** - Industry or academic group conducting research
7. **Interdisciplinary** - Combining multiple fields (CS, ME, EE, AI)
8. **Multidisciplinary** - Involving multiple disciplines without deep integration
9. **Embodied Intelligence** - Research area focusing on body-brain-environment coupling
10. **Sim-to-Real Transfer** - Research area bridging simulation and physical systems
11. **Foundation Models** - Large-scale pre-trained models for general-purpose capabilities
12. **Digital Twin** - High-fidelity virtual representation of physical system
13. **Grant Writing** - Process of securing research funding through proposals
14. **Publication Record** - Academic papers demonstrating research contributions
15. **Advisor/Mentor** - Faculty member guiding research and career development
16. **Thesis/Dissertation** - Original research contribution required for PhD
17. **Qualifying Exam** - Comprehensive examination testing breadth of knowledge
18. **Research Area** - Specialized domain of study (e.g., perception, control)
19. **Industry Partnership** - Collaboration between academia and companies
20. **Startup Ecosystem** - Network of early-stage companies and investors

## Section 12: Summary - Key Takeaways

**Word Count**: 400-600 words
**Purpose**: Consolidate main insights and actionable advice.

### Key Takeaways (10-15 points):
1. Multiple valid pathways exist - no single "right" way to enter the field
2. Interdisciplinary thinking is essential - combine CS, ME, EE, AI knowledge
3. Both physical and simulation research are valuable - consider integrated approach
4. PhD not always required - Master's + experience can lead to research roles
5. Research experience crucial - start early with projects, internships, publications
6. Network actively - conferences, collaborations, mentorships accelerate career
7. Funding available - fellowships, RA/TA positions, industry partnerships
8. Skills evolve - continuous learning essential in rapidly changing field
9. Publications matter - especially for academic careers
10. Industry vs. Academic - different cultures, choose based on values
11. Startups offer unique opportunities - early equity, rapid growth, diverse experience
12. Geographic flexibility - top programs and companies worldwide
13. Foundation models changing field - stay current with latest advances
14. Safety and ethics important - responsible research practices essential
15. Personal fit matters - choose pathway aligned with interests and lifestyle

### Common Mistakes:
- Waiting too long to start research experience
- Focusing only on coursework, ignoring research
- Not networking at conferences
- Choosing program based on ranking alone, ignoring research fit
- Neglecting either physical OR simulation (should understand both)

### Practical Tips:
- Start building portfolio early (projects, GitHub, publications)
- Reach out to potential advisors before applying
- Attend conferences even as undergraduate
- Consider industry internships during graduate study
- Develop both technical and communication skills
- Join professional organizations (IEEE RAS, ACM)
